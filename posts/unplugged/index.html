Unplugged

Posted on Sep 28, 2020
tl;dr: Some fresh air will do wonders for your productivity and well-being.

As entrepreneurs, startup founders, or whatever you call yourself, we often forget the importance of taking some time to recharge. These days we’re all so caught up in keeping up. We’re always checking our electronic devices for the latest updates, due dates, and meeting dates. In the fast-paced whirlwind of tasks we have to get through each day, it can be useful to remind ourselves that it’s okay to slow down and regroup every now and again. In fact, by slowing down you may just increase your own productivity in the process (counterintuitive, I know).

This week I’m taking some time for myself – something I haven’t done in a while – by trekking out to the rural mountains of southern Tennessee. My home for the week is a cabin tucked so far back into the wilderness that even Ron Swanson would be green with envy.

The Mintor team has been working so hard lately to get our MVP production-ready, and I was starting to feel the effects. Late nights, long weekends, and back-to-back meetings isn’t a sustainable way to work. Of course, sometimes it is necessary in short sprints to accomplish a specific goal or fulfill an upcoming deadline. But some recuperative time can do wonders when the work is done.

It’s not always easy to make ourselves step away when there’s always a backlog of tasks looming over your head. Below are some reasons why I stand by the need to ‘unplug’ regularly and balance things out. They’re simply observational, but I think these things would apply to anyone living this fast-paced ‘startup life’.

Decrease Anxiety and Stress

Even in the second day of the trip I’m noticing a severe decrease in anxiety and stress. Typically I’d be a frantic madman flitting from meeting to meeting, task to task, frequently code-switching (sometimes literally) between projects. Now that I’ve had some time to get some fresh air and gather my thoughts, I find that my baseline stress level has taken a nosedive.

Many studies have proven the fact that being stuck behind our electronic screens all day results in a reduction in self-reported physical health, mental health, and life satisfaction. It is no wonder that taking a break from our phones now and again helps to tip the needle back in a positive direction.

Improve Your Overall Health

A natural result of disconnecting is that you’ll have to fill your time with other activities. For this week, this means hiking, sight-seeing, and a bit of nature photography – film, of course, rather than digital. I’ll post some of my favorite photos after I develop them. This means that the time typically taken up by sitting and staring at a computer or phone screen is now usurped by healthier, physical activities. Adding these to the mix is never a bad thing.

Clear Your Mind

Indoors, your body works harder to provide the amount of oxygen it really needs. This causes an elevation in blood pressure. There are also studies out there which show that oxygen levels in the brain are correlated with serotonin levels. Serotonin affects your mood, memory, appetite, and many other essential processes. So, getting yourself outdoors can scientifically help to clear your head and reset your body.

There is also something to be said for the way natural light normalizes your sleep schedule. Once you start getting a proper night’s rest, you’ll notice a significant increase in energy and motivation.

I go to nature to be soothed and healed, and to have my sense put in order. – John Burroughs

In Conclusion,

It may not seem like taking some time away from work would cause an increase in productivity. But, with all of the above effects (among others) combined, it really is the best thing for your overall health once in a while. I know it has been for me, and I’m looking forward to the rest of this retreat.

As always, you can also follow me on Twitter as I continue to document my journey.
